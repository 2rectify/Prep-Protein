Beef Rendang

Slow-braised beef in a deeply spiced coconut paste until fall-apart tender. One of the best meal prep curries you can make.

What You Need

How To Make It
Method
1
Brown the beef
Dice the beef chuck into roughly 4cm cubes. Heat a large heavy-based pot or Dutch oven over high heat with a splash of oil. Brown the beef in batches until well seared on all sides. Don't rush this step — the colour builds the flavour base.
2
Add the onions
Dice the brown onions and add them to the pot once the beef is browned. Stir and cook for a few minutes until the onions soften and start to pick up colour.
3
Add the rendang paste
Add the full 100g of Ayam Rendang Curry Paste to the pot. Stir through and cook for 5 minutes, letting the paste toast and coat the beef and onions. The kitchen will smell incredible at this point.
4
Add the coconut milk
Pour in the 400ml of light coconut milk and stir everything together. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 5 minutes to combine. Make sure to scrape any paste off the bottom of the pot.
5
Low and slow in the oven
Cover with a lid and transfer to a preheated oven at 160°C (140°C fan). Cook for 3 hours until the beef is completely tender and falling apart. Check halfway and add a splash of water if needed. The sauce should thicken and reduce into a rich, dark coating.
🧊 Meal Prep Tips
✓ Divide into 10 containers once cooled. Keeps in the fridge for up to 5 days.
✓ Freezes perfectly ... portion into ziplock bags flat for easy stacking. Good for 3 months.
✓ Add 100g cooked white rice to each container when serving. Macros above already include this.
✓ The flavour actually deepens overnight — it's better on day 2 and 3.
