Weight Loss

What to Eat to Lose Weight

No fad diets. No gimmicks. Just how food actually works in your body and what you can do about it.

It Starts with Calories

So everything you eat has energy in it right. Thats what calories are, just a measurement of energy. Your body uses that energy to function, to move, to recover from your day. If you eat more than your body needs it stores the extra as fat. If you eat less it pulls from those fat stores to make up the difference. Thats literally the only way weight loss works. Every single diet that has ever worked, keto, low carb, whatever, they all work because they get you eating less energy than you burn. Thats it.

You dont need to count every calorie for the rest of your life or anything like that. But understanding the basics means you can make better choices without having to think too hard about it.

Not All Calories Are the Same Though

So a calorie is a calorie in terms of energy but the three types of food your body runs on, protein fat and carbs, they do completely different things once you eat them.

Protein has 4 calories per gram. Its what your body uses to build and repair muscle, keep your immune system running, and honestly its the thing that keeps you feeling full after a meal. Your body actually burns more energy digesting protein than anything else which means you get less usable energy from it than the label says. Of the three macros protein is also the hardest one for your body to convert into fat. So its kind of the best one all round.

Fat has 9 calories per gram which is more than double protein or carbs. Thats why fatty foods are so calorie dense and why its easy to overeat them without realising. But fat isnt the bad guy. Your body needs it to make hormones, absorb vitamins, protect your organs. You need some of it. The thing is those calories stack up quick so you just have to be a bit mindful of how much you are having.

Carbs have 4 calories per gram same as protein. They are basically your bodys go to for quick energy. When you eat carbs your body breaks them down into glucose which goes into your blood as blood sugar. And thats where things get interesting.

What Actually Happens When You Eat Carbs

So when your blood sugar goes up after eating carbs your body releases this hormone called insulin. Insulins job is basically to grab that sugar out of your blood and shuttle it into your cells so they can use it for energy. Totally normal totally fine.

The issue is when you eat heaps of carbs all the time especially the processed stuff like white bread, soft drinks, lollies, chips whatever. These hit your bloodstream fast and your blood sugar spikes hard. So your body pumps out a big dose of insulin to deal with it. And when insulin is high your body goes into storage mode. Its focused on clearing that sugar out of your blood and a lot of it ends up getting stored as fat. On top of that while insulin is elevated your body is way less likely to burn any existing fat for energy. So you are basically stuck in this loop of storing and never burning.

Now that doesnt mean carbs are evil or anything. It just means the type matters. Slower digesting carbs like sweet potato oats and rice release their energy more gradually so you dont get that massive spike and crash. You still get the fuel you need without the rollercoaster that leaves you starving again an hour later.

Why Protein Is the One Most People Dont Get Enough Of

Heres the thing. Most people eat plenty of carbs and fat without even trying. Its just in everything. Protein is the one you actually have to make an effort with and its the one that makes the biggest difference when youre trying to lose weight.

It keeps you feeling full way longer than carbs or fat so you end up eating less without even feeling like youre restricting. It helps your body hold onto muscle while you lose fat which is important because muscle is what keeps your metabolism ticking over. And because of that whole thermic effect thing where your body burns more energy digesting it you are basically getting a little bonus every time you eat it.

You dont need to obsess over tracking every gram but just being aware of whats in your food changes how you think about eating. Thats why every recipe on this site has a full macro breakdown. Once you start noticing how much protein fat and carbs are in your meals you just naturally start making better choices. It stops being a diet and starts being normal.

Where to Start

Honestly you dont need a complicated plan. Cook one or two big meals during the week, portion them into containers, chuck them in the freezer. Over time you build up this rotation of different meals and on any given night you just grab one out in the morning let it thaw in the fridge and heat it up when you get home. No cooking, no cleanup, no standing in the kitchen when you are already cooked from work. Just good food thats already done.
